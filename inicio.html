THE CHOLITOS OF THE ANDES


Located in the province of Juli, in the department of Puno, Chucuito is a unique destination on the shores of the majestic Lake Titicaca, the highest navigable lake in the world. Located about 3,820 meters above sea level, Chucuito offers an immersion in authentic Andean culture and the natural beauty of the region.

HISTORY OF JULI

The Dominicans were the first to arrive in Juli and with them their companions and Spaniards in search of a place to live. In 1553 the Convent of San Vicente was founded in Chucuito and in Juli the residence house for the religious was built. Starting in 1565, Juli became the main Dominican center in the area when the Convent of San Pedro Mártir was founded, displacing the Convent of San Vicente de Chucuito in hegemony and placing the newly founded Convent of San Santiago in Pomata under its command. El corregidor of Indians from Chucuito Don Jerónimo de Silva arrived in Juli, he demarcated the land for the same population, the squares and streets were laid out, the church site was expanded, he removed the natives to the suburbs, where they currently exist. the surnames Juli, Cariapaza, etc. and it was the residence of the first friars. On April 2, 1565, Mr. López García de Castro authorized the Dominicans to found the convent of San Pedro Mártir, on the ruins of the house of Cacique Cariapaza. Apparently they managed to start the construction of the first churches: San Pedro Mártir, La Asunción and San Juan. Then the missionaries of the Society of Jesus arrived at the end of 1576 and stayed until their expulsion in mid-September 1737. At the time of the Republic, on June 3, 1828, Juli was declared the capital of the province of Chucuito.


THE HISTORY OF THE PLACES

En Chucuito, el Templo de la Virgen de la Candelaria destaca como un importante sitio religioso con una rica arquitectura colonial. Las ruinas arqueológicas cercanas ofrecen una visión fascinante de las antiguas civilizaciones preincaicas e incaicas. Aunque no está en Chucuito, el lago Titicaca cercano es una atracción imperdible, famoso por sus vistas impresionantes y sus islas flotantes. La Plaza de Armas de Chucuito es un encantador punto de encuentro con arquitectura colonial y eventos locales. El Museo de Arte Precolombino ofrece una valiosa colección de artefactos históricos. Además, el mercado local es ideal para explorar productos auténticos y artesanías, y visitar las comunidades cercanas permite una inmersión profunda en la vida andina tradicional.
